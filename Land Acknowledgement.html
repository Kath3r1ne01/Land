Territory acknowledgment

Before the establishment of Canada, the Stó:lō people, known as the People of the River, inhabited the land where the University of the Fraser Valley (UFV) now stands.
Their territory spans the Fraser Valley and the lower Fraser Canyon in British Columbia, and their traditional language is Halq'eméylem, the upriver dialect.
UFV acknowledges and deeply respects the enduring contributions of Indigenous peoples to our community.The university is committed to supporting
Indigenous learners and embedding Indigenous perspectives and ways of knowing within its curriculum.
